Sandbox — Board is a triangular lattice mesh: vertices lie on an equilateral triangle grid; six triangles meet at every interior vertex (same as your reference images). Along a horizontal line through the plane, triangle orientation alternates ▲▼▲▼. Click cells ON (green). Each Fall 1 tick: a piece does not slide across an edge to a triangle of the opposite orientation unless there is no same-orientation step (rotation / tip). Lane step: among empty cells with the same kind whose centers are downhill in grav(rotStep), minimal sideways offset from the gravity line through the piece, then shortest step along gravity in that tier. Skips one mesh row in the same lattice column when the piece is not on the container edge (so the fall “jumps” the skipped orientation like 13→83, not 313→326). Wall tip: if no lane step, prefer a downhill rotation onto a mesh boundary cell (leftmost lateral among boundary flips). Interior tip: otherwise downhill rotation with “leftmost” lateral tie-break (min dot with rightHat). Wall pivot: when a vertex of the piece already touches the container polygon edge, prefer a downhill flip across an edge whose endpoints lie on that wall (before same-orientation lane skip). Lay-flat: on a mesh-boundary cell, if the container only supports the piece at vertices (not a full edge on the wall), prefer a downhill flip that increases full-edge contact on the container before lane slide. Log ⇔ board. Ids: #a→#b. Blue rN = lattice row index j. Crash demo: ?crash=1 or crash-fall-demo.json.
